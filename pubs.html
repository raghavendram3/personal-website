Surface-Activated Mechano-Catalysis for Ambient Conversion of Plastic Waste
Journal of the American Chemical Society (2024)
Hergesell, A. H., Baarslag, R. J., Seitzinger, C. L., Meena, R., Schara, P., Tomović, Ž., Li, G., Weckhuysen, B. M., & Vollmer, I.

Key takeaway: This research introduces a novel mechano-catalytic approach for plastic waste conversion that operates at ambient conditions, unlike traditional high-temperature methods. Surface activation of zirconia grinding spheres creates redox active sites that enable the formation of hydrocarbons from polypropylene without additional heating, offering a more sustainable recycling solution.

Revealing Active Sites and Reaction Pathways in Methane Non‐Oxidative Coupling over Iron‐Containing Zeolites
Angewandte Chemie - International Edition (2023)
Zhang, H., Bolshakov, A., Meena, R., Hou, Y., Plessers, D., Hensen, E. J. M., Bals, S., Abakumov, A. M., Gascon, J., & Camacho-Bunquin, J.

Key takeaway: This study identified the active sites in siliceous [Fe]zeolites that enable high selectivity for ethylene and ethane production from methane. The research demonstrated that isolated Fe³⁺ centers in the zeolite framework are reduced during the reaction to form the active sites, including Fe²⁺ species and Fe (oxy)carbides. This provides crucial insights for developing more efficient catalysts for natural gas conversion.

Toward the Rational Design of More Efficient Mo2C Catalysts for Hydrodeoxygenation–Mechanism and Descriptor Identification
ACS Catalysis (2023)
Meena, R., Bitter, J. H., Zuilhof, H., & Li, G.

Key takeaway: This comprehensive study identified the rate-determining step in butyric acid hydrodeoxygenation over molybdenum carbide (Mo₂C) catalysts as butanol dissociation. Through density functional theory and microkinetic modeling, the research demonstrated that Zr- and Nb-doped Mo₂C catalysts show enhanced catalytic activity, providing a pathway for designing more efficient and cost-effective alternatives to noble metal catalysts.

Ground-state properties of the narrowest zigzag graphene nanoribbon from quantum Monte Carlo and comparison with density functional theory
Journal of Chemical Physics (2022)
Casula, M., Meena, R., Sorella, S., & Mazzola, G.

Key takeaway: Using quantum Monte Carlo calculations, this study revealed significant antiferromagnetic stabilization energy and magnetization in the narrowest zigzag graphene nanoribbon. The findings suggest that antiferromagnetic correlations could persist above room temperature, which has important implications for potential applications in spintronics and quantum computing.

Comparison of RNC Coupling and CO Coupling Mediated by Cr–Cr Quintuple Bond and B–B Multiple Bonds: Main Group Metallomimetics
The Journal of Physical Chemistry A (2021)
Ghorai, S., Meena, R., Joseph, A. P., & Jemmis, E. D.

Key takeaway: This theoretical study compared the reductive coupling mechanisms of isocyanide and CO mediated by Cr-Cr quintuple bonds versus B-B multiple bonds. The research demonstrated that the difference in donor-acceptor capabilities of these ligands controls product distributions, with isocyanide reactions undergoing spin transitions while CO reactions remain on the singlet potential energy surface throughout.
