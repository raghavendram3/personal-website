Peer-Reviewed Publications

Surface-Activated Mechano-Catalysis for Ambient Conversion of Plastic Waste
Journal of the American Chemical Society (2024)
Hergesell, A. H., Baarslag, R. J., Seitzinger, C. L., Meena, R., Schara, P., Tomović, Ž., Li, G., Weckhuysen, B. M., & Vollmer, I.
Volume: 146, Issue: 38, Pages: 26139-26147 Published: September 25, 2024

This research demonstrated a novel mechano-catalytic strategy for plastic waste conversion at ambient conditions. The study showed that surface activation of zirconia grinding spheres creates redox active sites, enabling the formation of hydrocarbons from polypropylene without additional heating.

Revealing Active Sites and Reaction Pathways in Methane Non‐Oxidative Coupling over Iron‐Containing Zeolites
Angewandte Chemie - International Edition (2023)
Zhang, H., Bolshakov, A., Meena, R., Hou, Y., Plessers, D., Hensen, E. J. M., Bals, S., Abakumov, A. M., Gascon, J., & Camacho-Bunquin, J.
Volume: 62, Issue: 32, Article: e202306196 Published: July 18, 2023

This work explored methane non-oxidative coupling using siliceous [Fe]zeolites with MFI and CHA topologies. The study employed advanced spectroscopic techniques to elucidate active sites and intermediates, proposing a reaction network leading to polyaromatic species.

Toward the Rational Design of More Efficient Mo2C Catalysts for Hydrodeoxygenation–Mechanism and Descriptor Identification
ACS Catalysis (2023)
Meena, R., Bitter, J. H., Zuilhof, H., & Li, G.
Published: October 5, 2023

This work investigated the reaction mechanism of butyric acid hydrodeoxygenation over molybdenum carbide (Mo2C). The study identified rate-determining steps and explored heteroatom doping to enhance catalytic activity, providing strategies for rational catalyst design.

Ground-state properties of the narrowest zigzag graphene nanoribbon from quantum Monte Carlo and comparison with density functional theory
Journal of Chemical Physics (2022)
Casula, M., Meena, R., Sorella, S., & Mazzola, G.
Volume: 156, Issue: 8, Article: 084112 Published: February 28, 2022

This study used quantum Monte Carlo calculations to examine the ground-state properties of the narrowest zigzag graphene nanoribbon. The research revealed significant antiferromagnetic stabilization energy and magnetization, suggesting that antiferromagnetic correlations could persist above room temperature.

Comparison of RNC Coupling and CO Coupling Mediated by Cr–Cr Quintuple Bond and B–B Multiple Bonds: Main Group Metallomimetics
The Journal of Physical Chemistry A (2021)
Ghorai, S., Meena, R., Joseph, A. P., & Jemmis, E. D.
Volume: 125, Issue: 33, Pages: 7207-7216 Published: August 17, 2021

This research delved into the theoretical and computational chemistry aspects of Cr–Cr quintuple bonds and B–B multiple bonds, providing insights into main group metallomimetics.
